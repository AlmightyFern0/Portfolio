Nice to meet you.

I'm Lorenzo Marcomin, a new UX Designer on the block living halfway between north-western Italy and eastern Canada.

I graduated from the Polytechnic of Turin on the 1st of March, 2024, with a degree in Communication Design.

During my years at university, I focused on digital retail and web development. Although I’m still in the early stages of my career, I’m proud to say that I’ve already had the opportunity to work on real-life projects outside my curriculum, which have helped shape the digital identity of conventions, NGOs, and numerous institutions.

01

Accessible

In a digital space there are no excuses for barriers, I work with an “accessible first” methodology.

02

Inclusive

I strive to respect and celebrate diversity, and I try to make everyone feel valued and included within my projects.

03

Sustainable

I optimize my workflow and projects in order to generate the least amount possible of CO2 without sacrifing performance or security.

04

Impactful

I work on projects that have a meaningful positive impact on the world, such as advocating for human rights, funding medical research, and the like.

I strongly believe in leaving everything in a better state than when I arrived...

Before doing my job, I believe there are non-negotiable moral values based on social equality. I advocate for LGBTQ+ rights, mental health, disability rights, ecology, gender equality, respect for native communities and their protection. I'm interested in open-source software and the right to repair... In short, the usual intersectional feminism with a sprinkle of 'rights' in the tech field.

These values are deeply rooted in both the teachings I've received and my personal development. Being queer, I know what it means to feel excluded and to fight not only for my rights but to be able to live a normal everyday life. This is why I've decided that my work must contribute to improving the lives of the people it interfaces with.
